Northwest Neighborhood

These dorms are in a prime location for Business & SPEA majors. However, they are the furthest dorms from most other classes, so taking the bus is usually a must. The Northwest is in close proximity to Assembly Hall and Memorial Stadium and most athletes are put into these dorms. They are also known for their party atmoshpere (with the exception of Collins) and generally draw a more rowdy crowd as well as a higher percentage of students that go Greek.

Perfect for: Individuals who are outgoing and able to get work done in a somewhat noisier and more distracting environment. This is also a good area for "partiers", business majors, and those who are planning to rush a frat/sorority.

Pros

  • Proximity to athletics
  • Reputation of more sociable
  • Great for Business Majors

Cons

  • Typically noisier environment
  • Furthest from most classes
  • Least community kitchens

Dorms

  • Briscoe
  • Collins
  • Foster
  • McNutt

Central Neighborhood

The Central dorms are located around 10th street with almost every bus on campus having a stop right next to these dorms. Some close attractions include the Wells Library, SRSC (Student Rec Center), Jimmy Johns, Subway, A Total Tan, Red Mango (Frozen yogurt), and Pizza X. There is also a book store/spirit wear shop in Eigenmann.

Perfect for: The central dorms seem to be the most diverse, with a wide range of students. This area is a middle ground between the typical laid back personality type of the southeast dorms and the party atmosphere of the northwest.

Pros

  • Close to Library & Rec Center
  • All bus routes stop close by
  • Volleyball, tennis, & basketball

Cons

  • No dorms have private bathrooms

Dorms

  • Ashton
  • Eigenmann
  • Teter
  • Wright

Southeast Neighborhood

This neighborhood is located close to most freshman classes (along 3rd street) and is a good area for majors in Fine Arts and Music. The most popular nearby attractions include Mother Bears Pizza, TIS book store, and Jacob's school of Music. It is also located near the 3rd St. frats and sororities. It is the only neighborhood where there is dining on site in every single dorm.

Perfect for: Individuals who are laid back and want a relaxing quiet environment. Most fine arts and music majors choose these dorms due to their location and the fact that it is the only neighborhood with a music practice room in every dorm.

Pros

  • Proximity to most classes
  • Good study environment
  • Great for Arts & Music Majors

Cons

  • Reputation of less sociable
  • Far from athletics

Dorms

  • Forest
  • Read
  • Rose
  • Willkie
